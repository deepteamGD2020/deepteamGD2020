anastasiia umpeleva



laud softness: the art of gentle protest


Activism often conjures up quick transactional signing of petitions, loud and aggressive ways to demand justice outside on streets. For people from the Quiet World, most forms of traditional activism are uncomfortable, stressful, and sometimes terrifying. Is it even possible for introverts using their gentle voices to create big change? How can different personality types call for change? ‘Loud Softness: the Art of Gentle Protest’ invites you to the realm where craft marries activism. Through an extensive web archive and woven tapestry, Anastasia would like to manifest and draw attention to the idea that a creative gentle approach to activism can be even more world changing both personally and politically.

anna moschioni



domestic tales — odes to triviality


The scraggly charm bracelet you can’t find the courage to throw out, the random knick-knacks that sit on shelves, the calendars received every Christmas from your mother, the rocks picked up while walking, the old pile of rags mended over and over again... By letting owners speak about their mundane belongings and to what they remind them of, ‘Domestic Tales – Odes to Triviality’ collects these short stories, inviting the reader to reconsider the value of the often-diminished ordinary. The house turns into a theatre where oral narratives and memories come to life, depicting different aspects of people’s lives. The project dives into humans’ intimate relationships with the realm of objects through both auditory and written forms. It functions as a platform for sharing and showing the grounding role of domesticity.

berglind jóhannsdóttir



now and then


The past is full, full of eras distinguished by people and culture. The future is empty, but hot. ‘NOW AND THEN’ manipulates time by looking at the past and the future as parallel lines, lines of THENs, growing from NOW. This way a sense of past humanity is reflected into a heating future, lending it an ethos to better imagine what is at stake and when. Climate action is an action of respecting and loving the life and humanity on the Earth now and in the Future. It is now, in this critical decade, our responsibility to practice radical love!

brechje krah



Things and what we make of them


‘Things and what we make of them’ is a performance that functions as a metaphorical anthology for the topic of queerness, which is portrayed as a liquid matter defined by movement. ‘Things and what we make of them’ asks implicitly through visuals and explicitly through spoken word, what remains of fluidity when we attempt to acknowledge it. Does defining a liquid matter cause it to solidify? Does the ‘in between’ need borders for it to exist as such, in the same way that water needs a carrier in order to hold shape? As placeholders for fluid sexualities and genders, the work references varying matters such as semiotics and alchemy symbols, as well as the lived, physical experience. In showing a constant flux of material and movement, the project becomes queer itself; it is defined as it flows.

clara lezla



hypnosis of the stolen painting


When the real catches fire, borders are abolished, one zone—one space—crosses another and it is the whole world that becomes, to one degree or another, a gigantic theme park...

‘Hypnosis of the stolen painting’ is a video work questioning the nature of what surrounds us—the existence of imaginary and imagined worlds and spaces that are intertwined in what we call reality. Clara Lezla interviewed actors of these worlds or people who study them, and asked them to share their insights and intuitions about the different ways of unfolding fiction. The result is embodied in three visually distinct, yet echoing gates, built out of components of the worlds which they refer to. They can be seen as portals, trompe l’oeil, facades that harness the same principle of a cathedral frontispiece; that is to give visual information of the chemistry operating behind the walls—or in this case—of a fun fair attraction; to re appropriate an imagery and copy artefacts to a point at which we are disturbed, not knowing what we are looking at anymore... It makes us recall the cardboard cut out, the Hollywood decor, the setting. The gates are opened. It’s an invitation to enter. But what lies beyond? Nothing.... or everything. One gate leads into another, they belong to the same place; the simulacra of reality where the porosity of the borders between fiction and reality is always challenged.

dana doorenbos



true colors


On a daily basis we consume multiple grams of toxic ingredients trough the cosmetic products that we wear. With the project ‘True Colours’, Dana Doorenbos wants to bring light to the blind spots of the cosmetic industry, highlighting the deceptive ways in which they sell their products in order to disguise the truth. The project is divided into 3 sections: 1. The expectation the company creates through the invention of new words and product names; 2. The Promises that they make when advertising a product; and 3. The reality of what is actually in their products and what their effects are on the planet.
By dissecting existing advertisements strategies and stripping down all of the distracting glamour, she uncovers the tricks behind the language used by the cosmetic industry.

denise jansen



flaporen lexicon


A norm is a translation of the underlying values of a group or society into concrete rules of conduct and regulations. These particular rules can be written or unwritten. Norms guide social actions because people often unconsciously adapt their behaviour to the norms of the situation. The Flaporen Lexicon is a linguistic inventory that analyses the Dutch word ’flaporen’, better known as ‘stick-out ears’; it is a collection of fragments of written texts concerning stick-out ears. What are the negative and positive connotations of the dutch word ‘stick-out ears’? In The Flaporen Lexicon, you will discover the history, evolution and relationship between ‘flaporen’ and judgmental words, which guided the creation of an accompanying fictional cosmetic ear product.

edward dżułaj



eden apples


In his work, Edward Dżułaj is stressing the danger of far-right notions spread in Poland by political parties like the ONR. ONR—which means National Radical Camp—was founded in 1934. It is a far-right Polish organization with doctrines stemming from pre-Second World War fascism. For his graduation project, Edward created his own Private Press armed with a scalpel and an airbrush, with which he manually censored all of the content created by the ONR spanning across the last 86 years. By doing so, he changes the frequency of the voice of the far-right and in turn removes them from history. The process and outcome of the act of censoring are documented in the form of a book, several posters and a web extension.

emily anderson



particulate matters


Is there a better feeling than breathing clean, fresh air straight out of the Dyson Pure Cool TP01?
As clean air becomes scarcer, more products claiming to restore air quality are entering the market. ‘Particulate Matters’ explores the commodification of clean air by studying the language of air-purifier commercials. In this project, CGI mascots of ‘good’ and ‘bad’ air particles have been directly extracted from different air-purifier commercials. The air particles have been personified with scripted speaking roles. By re-contextualizing the virtually branded air-particles, ‘Particulate Matters’ aims to reveal the overlapping lexicons of cleanliness and right-wing speech and highlight the sombre reality that air purifiers are masking.

fien leeflang



eerste hulp bij ongemak (first aid for inconvenience)


Eerste Hulp Bij Ongemak (First Aid for Inconvenience) is an audiovisual series about the social inconvenience that lingers around the discourse on being invisibly different. It displays the perplexity that unfolds during daily conversations and the linguistic tools that people create to tackle them. Fien conducted in-depth field research on the interspace that exists around and between labels, diagnoses, (physical) disabilities, and further differentiations from the social norm. As a result, she highlights the overlaps across different personal experiences that find their origins in this interspace. The work functions as a documentation of this overlap and as a platform that offers recognition and support to these invisible groups.

golshan azadokht



beginnings without endings


Once again, Iran is facing a new wave of protests, this time sparked by the rising price of gasoline. These protests quickly became an outlet for broader frustrations: the joyless religious dictatorship. ‘Beginnings Without Endings’ introduces a timeline of these events inspired by flags that are displayed in Iran during Ashura festivals, flags that traditionally commemorate Hussain’s martyrdom, sayings of the prophet, verses from the Quran, or quotes from Khomeini and other important figures. These phrases are replaced by the slogans and phrases used by the protesters who have been shut down by the regime. The banners serve as a sign of grief, serving as an expression of sadness tied to these events, manifesting the on-going anger aimed towards the Iranian government.

hyeonjeong kim



the global k


Along with the development of South Korea’s modernity, K-Pop’s territory has been expanding to other parts of the world. Strongly established as a cultural product, K-Pop is popularised by and for the global audience of today, altering the definition of globalness with the same ideology and power structure as in the colonial past. With ‘The Global K’, Kihyeon visualises the hybridity of K-Pop culture with an aim to question its origins and motives aided by the nationalistic mentality and capitalistic drive. At the same time, the project acts as a personal exploration of a balance between celebration and contemplation of her own culture.

kiki coster



offspring


Prenatal screening has been standardized in many countries to detect potential medical defects. These developments have steadily reduced the number of hereditary diseases and conditions. Kiki, who herself has Cystic Fibrosis—one of the diseases eradicated at a rapid rate by current developments—draws on personal experience to investigate the tension between the clinical and impersonal ways the medical field deals with prenatal testing: the unrealistic, happy faced, pastel-colored graphic design that is used to advertise such tests, and the raw and often emotional realities of dealing with such decisions.

laura brouwer



biblically responsible investing


‘Biblically Responsible Investing’ by Laura Brouwer is a video installation and mixed media sculpture showing the relevance of religion’s entry into the daily practice of global financing. Biblically Responsible Investing (BRI) is a Christian investment approach that aligns with the investor’s faith and biblical beliefs. Christian investors exclude companies from their investment portfolios that are involved in affairs such as abortion, contraception, and the LGBTQ community. Indirectly, God is the portfolio manager. Laura did extensive research and discovered open-source BRI databases and screening tools to scan for so-called ‘sin stocks’. In these databases, Christian portfolio managers are constantly keeping track of companies’ violations and update reports with detailed descriptions of infringements. The data of this research is reflected in the video installation and mixed media sculpture.
