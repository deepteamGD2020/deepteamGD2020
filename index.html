Anastasiia Umpeleva

Loud Softness: the Art of Gentle Protest. Activism often conjures up quick transactional signing of petitions, loud and aggressive ways to demand justice outside on streets. For people from the Quiet World most forms of traditional activism are uncomfortable, stressful, and sometimes terrifying. Is it even possible for introverts using their gentle voices to create big change? How different personality types call for change?“ Loud Softness: the Art of Gentle Protest" invites you to the place where craft marry activism. Through the web archive and tapestry Anastasia would like to manifest and draw attention to the idea that a creative gentle approach to activism can be even more world-changing both personally and politically.

Anna Moschioni

Domestic Tales – Odes to Triviality...
The scraggly charm bracelet you can’t find the courage to throw out, the random knick-knacks that deposited on the shelves, the calendars received every Christmas from your mother, the rocks picked up while walking, the old pile of rags mended over and over again... By letting owners speak about their mundane belongings and to what they remind them of, Domestic Tales – Odes to Triviality is a collection of short stories that invites the reader to reconsider the value of the often diminished ordinary. The house turns into a theatre where oral narratives and memories come to life, depicting different aspects of people’s lives. The project dives into humans’ intimate relations with the realm of objects through both audio and written form. It functions as a platform for sharing and shows the grounding role of domesticity.

Brechje Krah

Things and what we make of them (Brechje Krah, Amsterdam 1993) is a performance that functions as a metaphorical anthology for the topic of queerness –itportrayed as a liquid matter, defined by movement. Thingsasks implicitly, through visuals, and explicitly, through spoken word, what remainsof fluidity when we attempt to acknowledge it. Does defining a liquid matter cause it to solidify? Does the ‘in between’ needborders for it to exist as such, like water needs a carrier to hold shape?As placeholders for fluid sexualities and genders, thework references varying matters such as semiotics and alchemy symbols, as well as the lived, physical experience. In showing a constant flux of material and movement, the project becomes queer itself; it is defined as it flows.

Clara Lezla

When the real catches fire, it’s the abolition of borders, one zone, one space crosses another and it is the whole world that is, to one degree or another, a gigantic theme park... «Hypnosis of the stolen painting» is a video work questioning the nature of what surround us; the existence of imaginary and imagined worlds and spaces intertwined in what we call reality. Clara Lezla interviewed some of the actors of these worlds or those studying them, and ask them to share their insights and intuitions about the different ways of unfolding fiction...The result is embodied in three visually distinct, yet echoing gates, fabricated with the components of the world they refer to. They can be seen as portals, trompe l’oeil, facades that use the same principle of a cathedral frontispiece; that is to give visual informations of the chemistry operating behind the walls, or of a fun fair attraction; to re appropriate an imagery and copy artifacts to the point we are disturbed, not knowing what we are looking at anymore... It recalls the card board cut out, the Hollywood decor, the setting.The gates are opened. It’s an invitation to cross. But behind? nothing.... or everything. One gate leading to another, they belong to the same place; the simulacra of reality where the porosity of the borders between fiction and reality is always challenged.

Dana Doorenbos

True Colors
On a daily basis we consume multiple grams of toxic ingredients, trough the cosmetic products that we wear. With the project True Colours (Dana Doorenbos) wants to show the audience the blind spots of the cosmetic industry, and the way they sell their products and disguise the truth.The project is divided into 3 sections. By dissecting existing advertisements strategies and stripping down all of the distractions (and temptations), she uncovers the tricks behind the language that the cosmetic industry created. For the Commison:
Explanation of what each project stands for:
1. The expectation the company creates with (the self invented) words and product names.
2. The Promise they make when advertising the product.
3. And the reality of what is actually in their products and what is does to our planet.

Golshan Azadokht

Once again, Iran is facing an uprising wave of protests, it started protesting the rise of the price of gasoline. These protests quickly became an outlet for broader frustrations; The joyless religious dictatorship. Beginnings without endings, introduces a timeline of these events inspired by flags that are displayed in Iran during Ashura festivals, those flags are normally commemorating Hussain’s martyrdom, sayings of the prophet, verses from the Quran or quotes from Khomeini and other important figures. These phrases are replaced by the slogans and phrases used by the protesters who have been shut by the regime. The banners are a sign of grief to express the sadness of these events indicating the ongoing anger toward the Iranian government.

Laura Brouwer

Biblically Responsible Investing
by Laura Brouwer (NL, 1995) is a video installation and a mixed media sculpture, showing the relevance when religion enters the daily practice of global financing. Biblically Responsible Investing (BRI) is a Christian investment approach that aligns withthe investor’s faith and biblical beliefs. Christian investors exclude companies from their investment portfolios that are involved in affairs such as abortion, contraception, and the LGBTQ community. Indirectly God is the portfolio manager.Laura did extensive research and discovered open-source BRI databases and screening tools to scan for so-called ‘sin stocks’. In these databases, Christian portfolio managers are constantly keeping track of companies’ violations and update reports with detailed descriptions of infringements. The data of this research is reflected in the video installation and a mixed media sculpture.

Linda van Houtum

“Remnants of Digital Ephemerality” is a layered digital landscape painting, and a series of engraved marble fragments by Linda van Houtum, displaying the transience of the digital image. Through the use of a technique, programmed to compare compressed imagery and extract the generated loss, the scenery transforms into a reversed painting, using compression as the paintbrush.

The colourful landscape of transience exists out of multiple layers compiled from a collection of different compressions using various methods of preservation, showing the unique depiction of visual elements. By transforming compression into a tool of creation, the remnants of the images are morphed into a landscape, and therefore become lossless and everlasting.

CALCULATED COMBUSTION
As opposed to spontaneous combustion, CALCULATED COMBUSTION is not only controlled, it is fabricated through simulation. Created to mimic real life physics, calculated combustion has very different purpose. Imitating a physics phenomenon to a point where only aesthetics remain. Aesthetics that have been sensationalised by humankind. Through my work I want to create an experience where the viewer is confronted with this fascination, creating a tension between contemplation and morbid curiosity.

Nicolai Schmelling

Pictures of Images Nicolai Schmelling (DK, 1994) works experimentally with image across the fields of graphic design, illustration and moving image.Their graduation work Pictures of Images is part of an ongoing research on image materiality. The work springs from the question: If an image is an object of knowledge, which knowledge is that exactly?Through a carefully developed technique, mundane video material becomes abstracted. The movements of the person guiding the imaging device smudge the image and paint a picture. The most consistent parts of the image remain recognizable. However, the blurred periphery, seemingly animated and alive, becomes the new point of interest, shifting what is knowable from the image to something beyond the frame and surface.As a guide to critically observing one’s own image making, Nicolai has formulated a written meditation, exhibited alongside the video displays as a resource. After rhythmically leading the reader to be attentive of their imaging device, the text ends with the encouragements: Make an image. What is your image a document of? You don’t have to look at it. Make a picture of your image.

Nicoletta Radice

MemoryBot
MemoryBot - a bot for memory back-up and memory exchangeWhen social isolation is required for the best of the community, can we make use of our collective memories as a tool to defeat loneliness? In these last months, Nicoletta Radice took upon a challenge which transformed into a profound journey exploring the construction of a chatbot. By feeding the bot her own memories, and memories of other people, Nicoletta created MemoryBot -a bot for memory back-up and memory exchange. MemoryBot is a fictitious web application, which you can interact with. It is a memory storage, specifically created to generate new memories in which the user can linger into. MemoryBot is not a replacement for human contact, but instead it is a speculative solution for aid and support, an exploration in the creation of artificial emotions. A project where human emotions and memories are mixed up by an emotionless machine.

153 Days
By looking at the conditions of a non-EU citizen who is confronted with when presenting oneself to the policy, S. Sophia de Jong

Dope Show
Today scientists claim to have found that GDF-8 Myostatin, a protein that regulates the amount of muscle mass that you can gain, if it’s genetically manipulated to lack, muscles can grow as twice as naturally possible and body fat is halved. The project is a speculative and critical video-poem about the dark side of a female’s bodybuilding journey seeking to become as muscular as possible with the help of CRISPR-Cas9, a novel and permanent genome editing technology. Tilius So

“I am you & you are me & you are we & we are all water”
In a film installation “I am you & you are me & you are we & we are all water”, Tilius Sodeika explores the human perception of nature in a speculative form. Tilius analyses how the relation with nature will be affected by the changing movement of water due to climate change, by the colonisation and purification of human habitat due to sea level rise and by attempt to communicate with water. The first part of the film is an interview Tilius had with Teun Terpstra, who is a researcher of climate adaptation, flood risk psychology, resilience of social and infrastructural networks and innovation development. Teun is an expert in how human perception of nature is constructed, which is explored in the interview. The second part of the film is exploring the sumblime of nature through a visual and audio representation of how the human land is being colonised by water. The visuals were creater using dynamic water qualities to result in a hyper representation of the consequences of climate crisis. Third part of the film is an exploration to why non-human actants, like water, need to have a voice in order for a dialogue on climate adaptation to broaden up. The film is designed to be viewed from multiple perspective angles and played on an island screen. The audience is invited to experience the film from their individual perspective, which allows the interaction with the narrative. This way the viewer contemplates over the vision of their relation with nature without being restrained to the conventional static story telling approach, which provides the audience with space for a fresh dialogue on a meaningful relation with nature to happen.

Trang Ha

The New Pangasius
If the world’s water is transforming, so are the fish. When enough fishes die, their souls will gather and wake us up at night. The panoramic map of The New Pangasius is a constellation of both found and made materials speaking of the highly commercialized and cultivated species of catfish in Vietnam, the Pangasius. Through the use of animated folktales, witty conversations, sketchy found footages and plasticky virtual objects, the project tries, without the need of scientific precision, to prove and reveal the complexities surrounding this chain supplies—“the dissociation of brand from factory”, “the relaxation of borders to trade, and the global consolidation of corporate power.”
